2025 U.S.–China Rhodes Scholar Dialogue

Navigating the Complexity of U.S.–China Relations Through the Lenses of Geopolitics, Economics, and Society

Agenda & Registration

Event Overview

This dialogue is initiated and hosted by Rhodes Scholars from China. Bringing together scholars, policy practitioners, business leaders, and cultural observers—many of whom are Rhodes alumni—the series explores U.S.–China relations across Geopolitics, Economics & Technology, and Society & Culture. Marking the 10th anniversary of the Rhodes Scholarship in China, the event aims for rigorous, respectful, forward-looking conversation.

Panels

Panel 1 — Bridging Lives and Lessons: Education, Everyday Experience, and Understanding Between China and the U.S.

Date & Time
Sat, Nov 15 · 9:00–10:30 PM (ET)
Guest
Peter Hessler (Missouri & Mansfield, 1992)
Discussants
Zhixin Wan (China & St Peter’s, 2022); Ryan Yan (China & Brasenose, 2020)
Show full description & bios

Description: Peter Hessler reflects on decades of reporting in China—how classroom life, migration, and everyday narratives illuminate larger social change in both countries, and how storytelling can bridge understanding between two societies often framed as rivals.

Peter Hessler

Author of the acclaimed “China Trilogy”—River Town (Kiriyama Prize), Oracle Bones (National Book Award finalist), and Country Driving—and Other Rivers: A Chinese Education (2024). MacArthur Fellow (2011); Rhodes Scholar (Missouri & Mansfield, 1992). Hessler’s work has been translated widely, with bestsellers in mainland China.

Zhixin Wan

Rhodes Scholar; PhD candidate in Anthropology, University of Oxford. Research focuses on disability inclusion in China. Previously earned an M.A. in International Journalism (Tsinghua) and a B.A. in English (Jiangxi Normal University).

Ryan Yan

PhD student in Psychology at Stanford; Rhodes Scholar. Holds an MSc in Clinical & Therapeutic Neuroscience and an MSc (Res) in Psychiatry (Oxford), and a B.S. in Applied Psychology (Nanjing University).

Panel 2 — Complexity Beyond Tension: Challenges and Opportunities in China Studies

Date & Time
Sun, Nov 16 · 9:00–10:30 AM (ET)
Guest
Michael A. Szonyi (Ontario & Merton, 1990)
Discussant
Xiaorui Zhou (China & Pembroke, 2020)
Show full description & bios

Description: A discussion of how field-based research and local history reshape our understanding of China’s society and state, the opportunities and challenges of studying China today, and how China studies engages broader debates in the social sciences and humanities.

Michael A. Szonyi

Professor of Chinese History and former Director, Fairbank Center for Chinese Studies (Harvard). Social historian of late imperial and modern China. Books include The Art of Being Governed (Princeton, 2017), Cold War Island, Practicing Kinship, and A Companion to Chinese History. Rhodes Scholar (Ontario & Merton, 1990).

Xiaorui Zhou

PhD candidate in History at Harvard; Rhodes Scholar (China & Pembroke, 2020). Researches women’s history in the Second Sino–Japanese War, focusing on relief work and everyday experiences. Holds degrees from NUS, Sciences Po, and Oxford.

Jake Sullivan
